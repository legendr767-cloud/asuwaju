The Day We Reached Ikoto: How a Simple Water Outreach Saved a Community From a Bigger Problem

Location: Ikoto Community, Ogun State

Ikoto, Ogun State — Last week, our team visited Ikoto Community for what was supposed to be a simple water distribution outreach. But what we witnessed opened our eyes to a much deeper problem — the silent crisis hidden in rural communities.

A Community Living on Contaminated Water

We noticed children fetching water from a small brownish stream behind the houses. When one woman was asked about it, she said:

“This is the only water we have. We boil it sometimes… but we still fall sick.”

We later learned that:

  • The nearest clean borehole was 3 km away.
  • Families depend on the dirty stream for cooking and bathing.
  • Children frequently suffer from stomach pains and diarrhea.
  • Some families spend half their income treating water-related illnesses.

A Child Who Couldn't Stand

We met a 7-year-old boy named Seyi who had been vomiting for two days due to bad water. He quietly said:

“Mummy, I don’t want to drink that water again.”

How Our Team Responded

  • Distributed clean drinking water to all households.
  • Shared health tips for safe water storage.
  • Provided ORS to children with symptoms.
  • Registered Ikoto into our Clean Water Program.
  • Began plans for a community borehole project.

An elderly man held a bottle of clean water and said:

“I am 62… I have never held water this clean.”

Why This Matters

This visit reminded us that communities like Ikoto are not asking for much — just the basic right to clean water.

Our Promise to Ikoto

  • Make clean water accessible to more communities.
  • Provide ongoing health education.
  • Support children vulnerable to waterborne diseases.
  • Partner with donors for sustainable water projects.

A Call to Action

Your support means healthier children, stronger families, and safer communities. Together, we can build a future where no child drinks from a dirty stream.
