A Morning in Ijebu-Ode That Changed Everything: Why Our Community Needs Us

A Morning in Ijebu-Ode That Changed Everything: Why Our Community Needs Us
Ijebu-Ode, Ogun State — Published: [add date]

Last month, something happened that reminded us why our mission as an organization is not just important, but urgently necessary. It was a Thursday morning when our team visited Italupe area of Ijebu-Ode. Our original plan was to meet a headmistress to discuss how we could support her school with exercise books. But what we met on ground was more than we expected.

A Child Who Came to School Without a Book

As we stepped into Primary 3B, the teacher was teaching English. The children were repeating after her, but one boy at the back caught our attention. He wasn’t writing anything. He just stared at the blackboard.

His name was Ridwan, a quiet 9-year-old. When the teacher asked him why he wasn’t writing, he quietly whispered: “Ma… I don’t have book.” The whole class went silent.

Later, we discovered that Ridwan’s mother is a widow who sells vegetables at Oke-Aje market. She has three children, and some days they eat only once. Even buying ₦150 notes was a struggle for her. That day, Ridwan walked 35 minutes to school with no book, no pencil, and no hope of getting one soon.

This is not a special story. This is the normal reality for hundreds of children in our local government.

📌 The Bigger Problem

During our visit, we found out that:

  • Some students share one exercise book for three subjects.
  • Many come to school hungry.
  • Some teachers buy materials with their own small salaries.
  • Parents want to support their children, but poverty limits them.

One headmistress told us: “We try our best, but the children’s needs are more than what the school or parents can carry. Even one book can change a child's confidence.”

Why This Touched Us Deeply

Because we realized something: these children are not lazy. They are not careless. They are simply forgotten by society. A book looks like something small to many people, but to them, it is the beginning of a future. A child without a book today becomes an adult without opportunities tomorrow. And that is exactly why our NGO exists.

Where We Come In

After seeing the situation, our team immediately decided to:

  • Begin distributing exercise books across primary and junior secondary schools;
  • Support widows struggling to keep their children in school;
  • Build transparent community programs that truly reach the people who need help.

Our hope is simple: If we can support the children of today, we will build stronger leaders for tomorrow.

A Small Act, A Big Change

When we gave Ridwan a pack of notebooks and two pencils, he held them tightly and said something that made one of our volunteers tear up: “I will write everything now.” Sometimes, the help people need is not big— they just need someone who remembers them. And that is what we want to be for our community.

Our Commitment Moving Forward

This is only the beginning. We are planning:

  • Monthly school support drives
  • Materials for widows and vulnerable families
  • Community empowerment programs
  • Leadership training for youths
  • Free Islamic education projects
  • And bigger humanitarian work as Allah gives us the strength

We believe change starts with small actions, done consistently.

A Call to Everyone

We are inviting members of the community, volunteers, donors, and partners to join us. You may not change the whole world— but you can change one child’s world. And sometimes, that is enough.
