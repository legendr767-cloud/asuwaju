When Light Returned to Ayetoro: A Community Outreach That Sparked New Hope

Posted on January 2025 • Location: Ayetoro, Ogun State

Ayetoro, Ogun State — Earlier this month, our team embarked on a humanitarian visit to Ayetoro, a semi-rural community facing multiple challenges affecting children, widows, and vulnerable families.

We expected a routine assessment. Instead, we discovered a community striving to survive in darkness — both literally and emotionally.

A Classroom Without Light

In one public school, Primary 5A had no electricity. The teacher told us:

“We haven’t had electricity for weeks. We still teach — because the children deserve to learn.”

Some students even shared candles for after-school lessons. This is their daily reality.

Meeting the Silent Heroes

We met Baba Adebiyi, who uses his small generator to power the homes of three widows. He said:

“If we don’t help each other, who will help us?”

The Story of Aisha

Aisha, a 12-year-old girl who dreams of becoming a nurse, walks 45 minutes to school every day. She said:

“If I stop going, I will fail. And I want to help sick people one day.”

What We Did Immediately

  • Delivered exercise books and writing materials
  • Distributed food packs to families
  • Supported widows with small funds for petty business
  • Provided rechargeable lamps to the school
  • Registered children into our Child Support Program

Our Next Steps for Ayetoro

  • Solar-powered learning kits
  • Monthly food-support distribution
  • Widow empowerment programs
  • Youth development initiatives
  • Back-to-school drives

A Call to Action

Change begins with small steps. Together, we can bring more light and hope to Ayetoro.
